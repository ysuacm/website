CSIS Problem Of The Week

Each week of the semester, YSU-ACM presents the CSIS Problem of the Week, powered by Kattis.

Rules and Regulations

In the interest of keeping the Problem of the Week fun for all involved, we ask you to follow several rules and regulations as noted below.

  • Problems will be posted each Monday morning. Submissions will be accepted up until the point when a new problem has been posted on the YSU-ACM website.
  • Problems will come from a variety of difficulty ratings, as specified by Kattis.
  • Any student is elligible to participate. However, work should be one's own, without any outside help from faculty or other students.
  • You may use any programming language allowed by Kattis. Your solution will be graded based on the following criteria:
    • Does it pass the test cases in Kattis?
    • How creative (note creative does not mean obfuscated) is the solution?
    Note that the above does not mention efficiency. Kattis employs restructions on submission (they must run within RAM and CPU time constraints), but efficiency is not graded beyond that.
  • Each week, we will appoint one winner whose name will be featured on the YSU-ACM website. The winner may be elligible for a small prize, though this has yet to be confirmed by the department.
